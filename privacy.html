Privacy Policy

Your personal data is like a rare orchid — handled with care, transparency, and never exploited.

Effective date: March 21, 2026 | Last reviewed: March 2026

Oak Moss World (“we”, “us”, “our”) respects your privacy and is committed to protecting your personal data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website (oakmossworld.com) — including our 78,900+ word gardening library, newsletters, and interactive features. We cultivate a relationship built on trust, aligned with GDPR, CCPA, and global privacy principles. Please read this policy carefully to understand our views and practices regarding your data.

Our privacy pledge: We never sell your personal information. Ever. Your data supports a richer gardening experience, not a commercial transaction behind your back.

1. Data Controller & Contact

Oak Moss World is the data controller responsible for your personal information. If you have any questions about this privacy policy or our data practices, please contact us:

  • Email: privacy@oakmossworld.com
  • Postal address: Oak Moss World, Attn: Data Protection, 1420 Evergreen Lane, Meadowvale Valley, CA 93726, USA
  • Phone: +1 (559) 778-9423 (Mon–Fri, 9am–4pm PT)

3. How We Collect Your Data

We use different methods to collect data from and about you:

  • Direct interactions: When you subscribe to “The Verdant Post” newsletter, create an account, leave comments, fill out a contact form, or request resources.
  • Automated technologies: As you browse our site, we may collect Technical and Usage Data via cookies, server logs, and similar tracking technologies. Please see our Cookies Policy for details.
  • Third-party sources: Occasionally analytics tools (Google Analytics, privacy-friendly metrics) help us understand reader interests, always under strict data processing agreements.
Cookies & similar tech: We use essential cookies for functionality and optional analytics cookies to improve our gardening content. You can manage preferences via our cookie banner. Learn more in Cookies Policy.

4. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal data only for lawful purposes, primarily to:

  • Deliver our gardening knowledge, newsletters, and personalized recommendations (if you opted in).
  • Improve and optimize Oak Moss World — analyzing which plant guides are most popular helps us create richer content.
  • Administer and protect our Site (troubleshooting, data analysis, fraud prevention).
  • Respond to inquiries, comments, or support requests.
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Service.

Where required by law, we rely on your consent (e.g., for non-essential cookies or direct marketing). You may withdraw consent at any time.

5. Sharing & Disclosure of Personal Data

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. Limited sharing occurs only in these scenarios:

  • Service providers: Trusted third parties who assist with email delivery (e.g., Mailchimp), analytics (Google Analytics with IP anonymization), and website hosting. All processors sign data protection agreements.
  • Legal requirements: If required by law, court order, or governmental regulation, we may disclose information to protect our rights, safety, or property.
  • Business transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, your data may be transferred (with notice and continued protection).

We never share your data with advertisers or data brokers.

6. International Data Transfers

Oak Moss World is based in the United States. If you access our site from outside the US, please be aware your information may be transferred to, stored, and processed in the US. We ensure appropriate safeguards (Standard Contractual Clauses) for data transferred from the EEA, UK, or Switzerland to maintain adequate protection.

7. Data Security & Retention

We implement industry-standard security measures — encryption, firewalls, access controls — to protect your personal data. However, no internet transmission is 100% secure. We retain your data only as long as necessary: newsletter subscriber data is kept until you unsubscribe; technical logs are retained for 26 months; account data until deletion request. After retention, data is securely anonymized or deleted.

8. Your Privacy Rights (GDPR, CCPA, and more)

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right to access – request a copy of data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification – correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure (“right to be forgotten”) – request deletion of your data (subject to legal exceptions).
  • Right to restrict processing – limit how we use your data.
  • Right to data portability – receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object – object to processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing.
  • Right to withdraw consent – anytime for consent-based processing.
  • Do Not Sell My Personal Information (CCPA) – Oak Moss World does not sell personal data; thus no “opt-out” required. If you are a California resident, you also have the right to non-discrimination for exercising your privacy rights.

To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@oakmossworld.com or via postal address. We will respond within 30 days. Verification may be required. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority (e.g., ICO in the UK, CNIL in France).

9. Email Marketing & Newsletters

When you subscribe to “The Verdant Post” (our weekly gardening digest), we collect your email and (optional) name. You may receive seasonal planting guides, exclusive articles, and occasional offers from trusted partners that align with our mission — only if you opted in. Every marketing email contains an unsubscribe link. Even after unsubscribing, we may retain your email on a suppression list to honor your request.

10. Children’s Privacy

Our website is intended for general audiences (gardening enthusiasts of all ages). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware that a child under 13 has provided us with personal data, we will delete it immediately. Parents/guardians may contact us to request removal.

11. Third-Party Links & Embedded Content

Our articles may contain links to external gardening suppliers, educational sites, or embedded YouTube videos. Those websites have their own privacy policies, and we encourage you to review them. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of third-party platforms.

Global compliance: We honor GDPR rights for EEA/UK visitors, CCPA for California residents, and similar frameworks. Our data map is regularly updated.

12. Cookies & Similar Tracking

We use cookies to enhance site functionality, analyze traffic, and remember preferences. You can manage cookies via your browser settings or our cookie consent tool. Essential cookies cannot be disabled because they enable core features like security. For more details, please see our dedicated Cookies Policy.

13. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal obligations. The “Effective date” at the top indicates when the latest version was posted. We encourage you to review this page periodically. Material changes will be notified via a prominent notice on the homepage or by email to subscribed users.

14. California Privacy Rights (CCPA) Supplement

If you are a California resident, California Civil Code Section 1798.83 permits you to request information regarding disclosure of personal information to third parties for direct marketing purposes. Oak Moss World does not disclose personal information to third parties for their direct marketing. For any CCPA-related inquiries (access, deletion, equal service), please email privacy@oakmossworld.com with “California Privacy Rights” in the subject line.

15. Data Breach Notification

In the unlikely event of a data breach that poses a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify affected individuals and relevant supervisory authorities within 72 hours where required by GDPR or other applicable laws.

Our promise: just as we nurture soil, we nurture trust. Your data fuels our mission to build the world's most generous gardening knowledge base — without ever compromising your privacy. Thank you for growing with us.
